Overview

From Regression to a Ranking Problem

Marketing budgets are constrained, yet a small fraction of customers drives most of the revenue. We reframe Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) prediction as a top-K ranking problem: identify the customers most likely to deliver the highest future revenue so retention spend can be concentrated on them.

The Business Problem

Why CLV Prediction Matters for VIP Targeting

  • Retailers operate with highly constrained marketing budgets → efficient allocation is essential.
  • Instead of generic blasting, prioritise high-value customers for exclusive VIP incentives.
  • VIP targeting shifts CLV from a pure regression task to a high-stakes ranking optimization problem.

Core business question

“Which top 10% of customers will capture the maximum future revenue share?”

All active customers → Robust CLV ranking model → Top-10% VIP group → Targeted campaign
Data Foundations

Online Retail II & the Zero-Inflation Challenge

Sourced from the UCI Machine Learning Repository: a UK-based non-store online retailer, December 2009 – December 2011. Accounts without a registered identifier are discarded to preserve sequence-tracking integrity.

Dataset at a glance

  • 805,549 transactions · 5,038 customers · 41 countries
  • $17.7M total revenue · median spend $899
  • Cleaning: keep Quantity>0 & Price>0, drop missing IDs, remove cancellations

Statistical obstacles

  • 42–67% future zero-spend rate (dormant / single-time shoppers)
  • Skewness coefficient 22.95 (Window 3) — heavy right tail
  • → Single-stage MSE regressors fail; a two-stage Hurdle model is required
Methodology

A Leakage-Free, Deployment-Oriented Pipeline

Strict walk-forward windows emulate production: features are computed only before the split, targets reside strictly after. Window 3 (18-month observation / 6-month horizon) is the primary deployment setting.

Two-stage Hurdle (proposed)

  • Stage 1 — classifier estimates return probability P(active) for all customers.
  • Stage 2 — regressor estimates positive spend E(revenue | active).
  • Predictions recombined multiplicatively → disentangles whether from how much.
  • CQR calibration wraps each prediction in a 95% reliable interval.
